Timeline

The following timeline shows how we expect events to unfold for phase 2. All dates should be considered at best tentative as Comcast and other outside factors may require adjustments.

Late October, 2012
The engineer from Comcast's local office will review our proposal and give final approval on the plan. At this time, we will also learn the actual cost for each route and which houses will be included.
Early November, 2012
We will kick off the fundraising efforts for each route. There will be a non-profit set up to accept donations, keep records, and issue money to Comcast when all is complete.
Late December, 2012
The Comcast corporate office will issue final budgetary approval for the project to the local office.
Late March, 2013
We must provide a single check to Comcast to cover all routes. The check will include the necessary amount for those routes which were able to raise sufficient money. For those routes which were not, the money they raised will be returned to the donors.
Early April, 2013
The engineer from the local Comcast office will begin planning for the actual installation.
Summer, 2013
Comcast and their subcontractors will install the cable and hardware needed for service.
Fall, 2013
Comcast's sales teams will start connecting customers and service will go live.

Mountain Broadband is a group of volunteers dedicated to bringing wired broadband coverage to the Loma Prieta region of the Santa Cruz mountains.
While the current best option for broadband appears to be Comcast, Mountain Broadband is not affiliated with Comcast, nor do we represent Comcast as the only solution.
