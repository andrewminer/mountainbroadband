We did it!

17-MAR-2013

It's official! Comcast has accepted our offer! This past Friday, the team delivered a check for $150,000 to Comcast and got their approval to proceed with all routes. As part of the funding, the county released $100,000 from the rural broadband fund which Comcast has been collecting for a total of $250,000. From there, Comcast discounted several routes which hadn't quite met their funding goals, and accepted the entire project as described on this website.

Please bear in mind that some routes have individual costs for trenching which are not included in this amount, so please contact your route leader if you were about to make a contribution—it may still be needed!

This has been a huge effort, and a lot of people have put in a lot of work to make all this happen! Many thanks to the Mountain Broadband team for their incredible efforts over the past year:

  • Project Leaders: Larry Lopp, Andrew Miner, Paul Wilkinson
  • Route A Leader: Greg Smith
  • Route B Leader: Grant Foster
  • Route C Leader: Holly Reed
  • Route D Leaders: Andrew Laird, Neil Kennedy, Sanjay Khandelwal
  • Route E Leader: Paul Wilkinson
  • Route G Leaders: Eiran Ben-Dashen, Jill Solitto

But, finally, and most importantly, a huge THANK YOU to all of our contributors! This is a community effort, and the success of the project ultimately comes from the generous people in the community who opened their wallets to make this project possible!

The next step is for Comcast to come out and do the actual engineering work necessary to get the ball rolling, so if you see Comcast trucks in the neighborhood, wave and give them a smile! We'll keep everyone informed as things progress.

So Now What?

02-MAR-2013

So, we're past February 28th, and no doubt the question on your mind is: "What happens now?" Good question. We've spoken with Comcast and given them a rough idea of where we are with fundraising, and we'll be talking with them again later this week to finalize the details of how/when we send them the money, and who gets included.

However, we're still about $26,000 short total across several routes (particularly along our three big routes: A, C and E), so if you're on the fence, please consider hopping down and joining the effort! Here's a letter from Shea Moss on route E who recently did:

I finally gave in and became a participant. I wasn't going to do it. I was dead against it. I was really outraged that we would have to pay for Comcast's infrastructure, when everyone I know just had to sign up and pay the monthly fee. Unfair, I thought.
But I went to the meeting on Saturday and listened to our neighbors who were participating. I went around and talked to individuals. Some felt like I do, but decided to invest in the community and in the future. One woman told me she is 64 and plans to move out in about 6 years. She knows she will have a hard time selling her house without internet access, and will get a much lower price. One young couple with an infant didn't want to have her son growing up without access to all the wonders of the internet. Others were trying to work at home more and communicate with their offices in the Valley. Some people work out of their homes and have very small businesses just getting by with what Verizon has to offer. Some people who were retired just wanted more entertainment, and to be able to watch movies at home. I thought, these people all have valid reasons, but, should I care?
I didn't give my check right then, but as I was driving home, I had to ask myself, why do I live here if I can't spend a little bit of money to support my community. I don't have little kids, but I still pay for schools and all that. What if the shoe were on the other foot? I'd be desperate to participate. And here's the thing, all you have to do is participate. If you don't want to throw one of the suggested amounts into the pot, just throw in what you would spend on a weekend in say, Yountville with dinner at the French Laundry, or a weekend fly fishing in Montana, or green fees for 4 at Pebble Beach, or whatever you can dream of. If you think of it that way, it won't hurt as much. And the benefits will last much longer. I think the County and Comcast are watching us, to see how many people really want better service. They want to give it to us, but we have to show we want it by putting down some 'serious money.'

We commend Shea for giving this some real thought, and choosing the long-term benefits of having wired broadband in our community. While we nail down the final details, we urge everyone else who's still thinking about it to do the same.
