Comcast Meeting

16-DEC-2012

Just before Thanksgiving, we were able to sit down with Comcast and discuss the details of our plan. We talked through the various routes, what would be involved, and got a pretty good picture of how Comcast is going to proceed. Here are the most important points of what we learned:

  • We will hear back on the budget for each route in early-mid January.
  • Funds will be due to Comcast April 1st (i.e., back to the origin schedule)
  • Where necessary, easement documents will be needed April 1st as well.
  • The standard hook-up fees for individual homes will be:
    • overhead line up to 300': $50
    • underground conduit up to 300': $50 (Comcast digs the trench)
    • underground conduit 300' to 500': $50 (owner digs the trench)
    • over 500': Comcast will assess individual cases

So, things are generally moving in a very good direction, and we now have a much more definite idea of when we'll be able to start the fundraising. We'll keep everyone informed as the time approaches!

Updated Timeline (again)

11-NOV-2012

We have another update from Comcast, and it's a little bit of bad news, but mostly good news. The bad news is that, due to the phase 1 build-out and other projects, they have not been able to review our proposed routes, and will not have costing information available for us until after the new year. They're going to start looking into our proposal in detail this coming week, and they've invited us to send a few people to talk with them about what we have in mind.

The good news is the Comcast corporate office has already given budgetary approval for the phase 2 build-out! This means that so long as we're able to raise our side of the money, the project is a go! The other piece of good news is that they won't need our portion of the money until July of next year, but construction is expected to begin around the same time.

This doesn't mean we're done yet, though. The planning team is going to use this time to make sure we're ready to go as soon as we get some solid numbers from Comcast in early January. Stay tuned, and we'll post further details as we get them.

Updated Timeline

30-SEP-2012

We have recently gotten an update from Comcast, and we now have a much more definite idea of what the timeline will be for fundraising, sending money to Comcast, the actual build-out, and when service will go live.

In short, we expect to have actual costs for the routes in late October, and we will need to turn over the money for the community portion to Comcast at the end of March, 2013. In between, we will be organizing fundraising efforts for each route to help them meet their targets.

We are also organizing a non-profit organization to accept and manage donations, and you will hear more from us when that is ready to go.

Welcome to Mountain Broadband

10-SEP-2012

This website provides information for anyone interested in the ongoing efforts to bring high-speed internet access to the Loma Prieta community in the Santa Cruz Mountains. This has been a multi-year, multi-phase project. Phase I of the project is nearly complete with service expected to come online in the next few weeks. Phase II is finishing the planning stage and is about to enter the fundraising stage.

Read the About section for more project history

The Phase II build out will be organized according to individual neighborhoods corresponding to the routes the cable will follow. Each neighborhood will need to pay for a portion of the cost for building out that route. Those routes that can raise the necessary funds will be included in the build out, while those that cannot, will not. To that end, we will be organizing a fundraising effort in each neighborhood in the next few weeks.

Examine the Routes and contact your route leader

It costs about $60,000 per mile to install the necessary cable and hardware for wired internet service and we have over 13 miles to cover, so this is going to be a huge project. It is also a huge investment with a number of far-reaching benefits.

Read about the Benefits of having wired, high-speed internet

Unfortunately, we won't have exact answers to many relevant questions of cost, schedule, service levels, etc. until we hear back from Comcast about our proposal. In the meantime however, we are here to share what information we do have, and get answers to your questions.

Read the F.A.Q. for more information

We're your neighbors, friends and family, and we all want wired, high-speed connections in our homes too. We're volunteering our time to try to make the most of this opportunity to get a faster, cheaper and more reliable connection. While Comcast is currently the best contender, we're not affiliated with them in any way. We just want the best service we can get for the best price. We hope we can count on your help.
